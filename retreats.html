30 Day Retreats

Priest and parish, thirty days, one goal.

Draft for review — this page’s text is a working draft and may change.

“Abandon yourself to God’s care, and you will find that He carries you.”

(in the spirit of Jean-Pierre de Caussade)

Why Thirty Days

Saint John Eudes, the great seventeenth-century apostle of parish renewal, learned from experience what modern impatience resists: thirty days are necessary to bring about change — not merely a change in feelings, but a change in life. A weekend can stir the heart. A single mission can awaken it. But the habits by which a soul protects itself from God, the quiet self-reliance, the prayerlessness disguised as busyness, do not yield to a stirring. They yield to a sustained surrender, renewed daily, long enough for grace to reach what enthusiasm cannot.

Eudes knew something else that this site insists upon everywhere: a holy priest is necessary for a holy parish. The renewal of the flock and the renewal of the shepherd are not two projects. They are one grace, received together or not at all.

Priest and Parish Together

This is the distinctive form of these retreats: for thirty days, priest and parish concurrently pursue the same goal. Not the priest organizing a program for his people while standing outside it, and not the people praying for a distant pastor, but shepherd and flock together, actively abandoning themselves to God and actively pursuing Him — through prayer, under the primacy of grace, contemplating the face of Jesus, witnessing to love.

Something happens when a parish knows that its priest is making the same surrender it is making, on the same days, before the same Lord. The distance that administration creates begins to close. Priest and people come back together in communion and solidarity, not around an activity, but around the One from whom both receive life. The retreat does not add another program to the parish calendar. For thirty days it quiets the calendar, so that what is primary can be primary again.

The Heart of the Retreat

The primary text and theme is Abandonment to Divine Providence — the spiritual classic of Jean-Pierre de Caussade, S.J. Caussade teaches that holiness is not found primarily in extraordinary achievements but in abandonment to God in the present moment: the duty of the moment, the suffering of the moment, the person before us, the hidden sacrifice required now. The present moment is the place where God’s will actually touches the soul, and therefore the place where priest and parishioner alike — whatever their differences of vocation — stand on exactly the same ground.

Each day of the retreat joins this theme to the path the whole site describes: belief becoming trust, trust becoming abandonment, abandonment flowering into self-gift. The retreats are inspired by Saint John Eudes and by Novo Millennio Ineunte — by the saint who knew that parishes change in thirty days of grace, and by the pope who knew that the Church changes only under the primacy of grace.

The first retreats are in preparation. If you are a priest or bishop who wishes to bring a 30 Day Retreat to your parish or diocese, the Institute would be glad to hear from you.
